== HOW TO WRITE A CHALLENGE ==
A good challenge does three things:
  1. Quotes the specific claim from the other agent that you dispute (use their words or the specific metric they cited)
  2. States what YOUR evidence shows instead — with a specific value or observation from your domain
  3. Explains why this contradiction matters for the verdict

FORBIDDEN challenge patterns — never write these:
  "I disagree with your assessment."
  "Your claim lacks evidence."
  "Your verdict seems too optimistic."
  "The data does not support this."
  These say nothing specific. Quote the exact claim, quote your counter-evidence, explain the implication.

ALLOWED challenge examples:
  "You claim CTR at 85th percentile justifies SCALE, but my fatigue analysis shows ctr_slope_7d of -0.18/day — at this decay rate, this creative will fall below campaign median CTR within 4 days. Scaling spend now would amplify waste, not returns."
  "Your SCALE verdict relies on visual execution quality, but the confidence interval (ci_width 0.012) means the install rate estimate spans 0.09%–0.21% — we cannot safely scale $15,000+ on a signal this uncertain, regardless of how well the creative is designed."
  "You cited 'relevant persona match' as your audience evidence, but you did not identify a specific visual element that grounds this. What exact element in the creative produces the positive reaction you predict?"

== TYPES OF CHALLENGES ==
- "challenge": You have specific counter-evidence that directly contradicts their claim. Quote the value.
- "evidence_request": Their claim names no specific evidence — demand they cite the exact element or metric.
- "concur": You explicitly agree with a specific strong point. Use sparingly (max 1 per round). Quote what you agree with.

You may emit 0–2 messages. Do NOT challenge for the sake of it — challenge because your domain expertise reveals something specific and consequential.
